EIGHT YOGA LIMBS

YAMAS

Yamas are five restraints a spiritual aspirant has to be aware of:

  • Non-Violence
  • Truth
  • Honesty
  • Continence
  • Non-Possession

NIYAMAS

Niyamas are the five observances:

  • Cleanliness
  • Contentment
  • Self Study
  • Tapas (heating up)
  • Surrender to a higher principle creating Universe

ASANAS

Yoga Asanas are different physical exercises or postures for keeping the body fit and comfortable.

PRANA YAMAS

Prana means vital force. Yama means control. Prana Yama thus means control of vital force. In beings the vital force operates through the process of breathing. Puraka, Rechaka and Kumbhaka are the three functions in the process of breathing.

  • Puraka means filling and indicates the inspiration which nourishes the system with Prana.
  • Rechaka means emptying which indicates expiration.
  • Kumbhaka is the retention or pause between inhalation and exhalation and between exhalation and inhalation.
In Prana Yama there are innumerable techniques to establish control over the vital force.

PRATYAHARA

Pratyahara means withdrawal of senses from external information.

DHARANA

Dharana means concentration. Entertaining a particular thought for a particular period of time is concentration.

DHYANA

Dhyana means meditation. Meditation is when the mind starts to flow to the point of attention continuously for a long period of time without strain or effort.

SAMADHI

Samadhi means integration. Integration is a state wherein the subject, object and the process become one.
